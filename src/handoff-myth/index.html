The handoff myth

It seems there's always software being worked on to remove designer/developed handoff, from Dreamweaver to more modern tools like Framer.

I've always felt these were worthy and interesting pursuits, often leading to greet tools (such as Webflow).

As I've worked more on product teams, I've also come to appreciate another perspective.

Visual design, user experience, motion and engineering aren't stages of a process with handoffs between - but rather perspectives to look at a product.

People with these skill sets work on a product simultaneously, with their unique perspective, using the software they're most comfortable with.

They constantly handoff between each other, and it's in these small, regular handoffs that I think the most effective improvements are done.

Over optimizing for a single handoff between people can lead to more difficulties, as the inevitable smaller updates continue to happen.

What might it look like to optimize for these small, regular handoffs?
  • Working on team cohesion at a people level
  • Tooling to help translate small parts between peoples preffered software
  • Defined patterns that are shared between areas
