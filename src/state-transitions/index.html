Motion as state transitions

When animating a video, you can think of an animations as a function of time. As time increases, like in a video player or your After Effects timeline, your animation progresses.

When doing motion for interfaces, your animations are driven by state, not time.

This is a fundamental change in the way you think as an animator.

A few differences that come to mind:

  • There will probably be more states to transition between then you think, look out for edge cases
  • Transitions can be intercepted or cancelled
  • You might need to take into account what triggered the state change, such as a tap vs gesture
